Houses or Museums?

Lina Bo Bardi, 1958

What should come first, houses or museums? Everything at once: the houses, the schools, the museums, the libraries. Urban Planning cannot ignore cultural issues.

Museum? What is a museum? In everyday life, when we want to describe a person, thing or idea that is outdated, not practical or useful, we often say “they belong in a museum.”

The modern museum has to be a didactic museum, able to marry conservation with the message that it is the art that must be highlighted, while everything else has a far more modest role.

Evidently written texts, brief and succinct, accompanied by photographs – in a sort of cinematographic commentary – are essential if the museum is to occupy a vital place.

First published in Diário de Notícias (Salvador, Bahia), 5 October 1958
